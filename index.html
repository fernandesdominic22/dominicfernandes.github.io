Matthew Lisondra

I am currently a Researcher of Robotics at Toronto Metropolitan University.

I do research at the Robotics and Computer Vision Laboratory (RCVL) directed by Dr. S. Saeedi, working on Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), State Estimation, Visual-Inertial Sensor Fusion (VIO), Computational Imaging, Computer Vision, Autonomous/Intelligent Systems Algorithms and next-generation vision sensors such as the Focal-Plane Sensor-Processor (FPSP) Chips.

I also do research at the Haptics and Telerobotics Laboratory (HapTel) directed by Dr. K. Zareinia, working on Image-Based Force Estimation in Medical Applications research.

As well, I am working at Rosor Exploration, on the team doing research and development of Rosor's Active Terrain Following (ATR) system. Rosor is a near-surface geoscientific drone inspection company, specializing in remotely piloted aircraft systems development and operations to carry out mineral exploration projects and investigate for new potential mining sites.

I am also teaching as the lead in Higher-Level Physics and Mathematics at the Department of Mathematics and Science at Virtute Innovation Academy. I have also taught K–12, secondary and university-level instruction at various private teaching companies in Western Canada BC, China and the US at UCLA, UC Berkeley, UCSD, among others.

I obtained my HBSc Honours Bachelor of Science, Physics Specialization at the University of Toronto (St. George). I collaborated with Dr. D. Jones of the Atmospheric Physics and Composition Modelling Group on Time Series Analysis on Increasing Average Global Temperature and Standardized Sea Level Pressure.

Research -- Robotics, Robot Perception, Robot Learning, Computer Vision and SLAM.
