Matthew Lisondra

I am currently a MS Researcher at Toronto Metropolitan University.

I do research at the Robotics and Computer Vision Laboratory (RCVL) directed by Prof. Sajad Saeedi, working on Visual-Inertial Sensor Fusion (VIO and SLAM), Autonomous Driving Algorithms and Focal-Plane Sensor-Processor (FPSP) Chips.

I also do research at the Haptics and Telerobotics Laboratory (HapTel) directed by Prof. Kourosh Zareinia, working on Image-Based Force Estimation in Medical Applications research.

I obtained my HBSc Honours Bachelor of Science, Physics and Mathematics -- Specialist at the University of Toronto (St. George). I collaborated with Prof. Dylan Jones of the Atmospheric Physics and Composition Modelling Group on Time Series Analysis on Increasing Average Global Temperature and Standardized Sea Level Pressure.

Research -- Robotics & Physics, AI and Computer Vision.

Specific areas of focus include:

  • Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)
  • State Estimation, Visual-Inertial Sensor Fusion (VIO)
  • Computational Imaging, Computer Vision
  • Autonomous/Intelligent Systems Algorithms
  • Machine Learning, DeepLearning and Reinforcement Learning
