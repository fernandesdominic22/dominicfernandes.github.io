Matthew Lisondra

I am currently a MS Researcher at Toronto Metropolitan University for the Robotics and Computer Vision Laboratory (RCVL) directed by Prof. Sajad Saeedi, and for the Haptics and Telerobotics Laboratory (HapTel) directed by Prof. Kourosh Zareinia. I have worked on Visual-Inertial Sensor Fusion (VIO and SLAM), Autonomous Driving Algorithms, Focal-Plane Sensor-Processor (FPSP) Chips, and Image-Based Force Estimation in Medical Applications research.

I obtained my HBSc Honours Bachelor of Science, Physics and Mathematics -- Specialist at the University of Toronto (St. George). I collaborated with Prof. Dylan Jones of the Atmospheric Physics and Composition Modelling Group on Time Series Analysis on Increasing Average Global Temperature and Standardized Sea Level Pressure.

My core research interest is at the intersection of Robotics & Physics, AI and Computer Vision. Specific areas of focus include:

  • Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)
  • Visual-Inertial Sensor Fusion (VIO)
  • Autonomous System Algorithms
  • Deep/Reinforcement Learning (D/RL)
